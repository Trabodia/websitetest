Marcos pushes PUV consolidation deadline to April 30

The deadline for the consolidation of public utility vehicles (PUV), initially due December 31, 2023, was granted a 3-month extension until April 30, 2024, as confirmed by the Presidential Communications Office (PCO) on Wednesday.

President Ferdinand Marcos Jr.’s decision on the lengthened PUV consolidation period was made following a recommendation from Transport Secretary Jaime J. Bautista.

‘’This extension is to give an opportunity to those who expressed intention to consolidate but did not make the previous cut-off.’’ PCO emphasized.

For now, the Land Transportation Franchising and Regulatory Board’s (LTFRB's) recently issued Memorandum Circular 2023-052 allows unconsolidated PUV drivers to operate until the end of January 2024, in light of continuing public transportation operations.

Transport officials state that those who failed to consolidate before the December 31 deadline will not be apprehended by authorities until January 31, 2024.
